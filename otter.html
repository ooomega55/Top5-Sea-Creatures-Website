Otter:

Information About Otters:

  • When otters dive, they seal their ears and nose.

  • They are often seen floating on their backs. Their thick fur traps air and makes them buoyant.

  • Otters are found almost all over the world and in many wet habitats, such freshwater rivers, lakes, oceans, coastlines and marshes.

  • A group of otters is called a raft, according to the San Diego Zoo. They are also nocturnal and hunt at night.

  • Sea otters eat a wide range of marine animals, including mussels, clams, urchins, abalone, crabs, snails and about 40 other marine species, equaling approximately 25 percent of their weight in food each day.
